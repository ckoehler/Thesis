\section{Amiguity Function}
The main tool to determine waveform performance is the Ambiguity Function (AF). It is defined as:

\begin{equation}
\label{AF}
  |\chi(\tau,f_d)| = | \int_{-\infty}^\infty{u(t) u^*(t+\tau) e^{j2\pi f_d t} dt}|
\end{equation}

, where $u(t)$ is the complex envelope of the signal, $\tau$ is the delay on the delay axis, and $f_d$
is the Doppler frequency shift in the Doppler axis.
We can clearly see that, for $f_d = 0$, the AF reduces to the Autocorrelation Function (ACF).


For a simple rectangular pulse of the form $u(t)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{T}} rect(\frac{t}{T})$, the AF is:
\begin{eqnarray}
  |\chi(\tau,f_d)| &=& | \int_{-\infty}^\infty{u(t) u^*(t+\tau) e^{j2\pi f_d t} dt}| \nonumber \\ 
  &=& 
  \begin{cases}
    \frac{1}{T} \int_{-\frac{T}{2} + \tau}^{\frac{T}{2}}{e^{j2\pi f_d t}dt} & \text{for $0 \leq \tau \leq T$} \nonumber \\
    \frac{1}{T} \int_{-\frac{T}{2}}^{\frac{T}{2} + \tau}{e^{j2\pi f_d t}dt} & \text{for $-T \leq \tau \le 0$} \nonumber \\
    0 & \text{otherwise} \nonumber \\
  \end{cases}
\end{eqnarray}

Evaluate the integral:

\begin{eqnarray}
  \frac{1}{T} \int_{-\frac{T}{2} + \tau}^{\frac{T}{2}}{e^{j2\pi f_d t}dt}
  &=& foo \nonumber \\ 
  &=& bar \nonumber
\end{eqnarray}
